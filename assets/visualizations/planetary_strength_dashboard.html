Key Insights

Planetary Strength Variations: In Vedic Astrology, planetary strength is not static but changes throughout the day, month, and year based on numerous factors including zodiacal positions, dignity (exaltation/debilitation), house placements, and temporal conditions. The visualizations show these continuous variations to help you identify optimal periods for important decisions.

Numerology's Discrete Nature: Unlike astrology's continuous system, numerology operates on discrete changes. Your Mulanka (birth number) changes only on specific dates, creating a distinct pattern that complements the astronomical variations shown in astrology. The comparison charts help identify periods when both systems are aligned.

Practical Application: When planning important events (business launches, marriages, travel), identify days when both numerology and astrology show strong support. The convergence of these independent systems often indicates particularly auspicious periods.

Technical Specifications

Data Source: Swiss Ephemeris (pyswisseph) provides high-precision astronomical data for accurate planetary positions and movements.

Calculation Method: Planetary strengths are calculated using Vedic astrology principles including dignity factors, temporal conditions, and aspection analysis. Numerology calculations follow traditional Vedic numerology (Anka Jyotish) systems.

Update Frequency: Charts are generated daily and cover a rolling 365-day period. For updated visualizations with current planetary positions, regenerate the charts using the provided Python script.

How to Interpret the Charts

Green Zones (75-100): Excellent periods for initiating new projects, making important decisions, and taking action. Planetary support is at its peak.

Blue Zones (50-75): Good support with favorable conditions. Suitable for most important activities and decisions.

Orange Zones (25-50): Weak support period. Use caution and avoid critical decisions if possible. Good for planning and preparation rather than action.

Red Zones (0-25): Poor support with challenging conditions. Avoid major decisions and actions. Focus on maintenance and low-risk activities.
